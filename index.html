I am an applied scientist at Amazon Web Services working on statistical methods for computer vision and Responsible AI. In 2022, I completed a PhD in Statistics at Carnegie Mellon University under the supervision of Alexandra Chouldechova and Zachary Lipton.

During the PhD, I interned at Microsoft Research working with Besmira Nushi, Kori Inkpen, and Eric Horvitz. I also spent seven months as a research fellow at the Partnership on AI with Alice Xiang. Prior to my graduate studies at CMU, I was a student at the Collegio Carlo Alberto and the University of Torino, where I was advised by Matteo Ruggiero. I received my undergraduate degree from the University of Padova, with an Erasmus exchange at the École Normale Supérieure de Cachan.

You can contact me at riccardofogliato [at] gmail [dot] com.

When I'm not injured, I run and log some miles (kms) on Strava.
